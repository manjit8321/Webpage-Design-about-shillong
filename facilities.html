Facilities in Shillong

Transport

Although well connected by road, Shillong has neither rail connections nor a proper air connection. Umroi Airport located 30 kilometres (19 mi) from the city centre has only limited flights. Recently regular flights have been made available for Shillong to Kolkata from July 2019.

Hospitals

Sub-district or Sub-divisional hospitals (SDHs) are below the district and above the block level (Community Health Centres) hospitals and also act as First Referral Units with bed strength ranging from 31 to 100. Specialist services are provided through these sub-district hospitals. These hospitals should play an important referral link between the Community Health Centres, Primary Health Centres and sub-centres.
District hospitals (DHs) with bed strengths ranging from 100 plus to 500 are an essential component of the district health system and function as a secondary level of health care which provides curative, preventive and promotive healthcare services to the people in the district. Every district is expected to have a district hospital linked with the public hospitals or health centres down below the district such as Sub-district or Sub-divisional hospitals, Community Health Centres, Primary Health Centres and Sub-centres.
State hospitals (SHs) with bed strengths ranging from 100 plus to 500 are an independent entity and provide specific services like Ganesh Das Mother and Child Hospital in Shillong, Reid Provincial Chest Hospital in Shillong and TB Hospital at Tura. They report directly to the state directorate and are autonomous in function.

  • Cinema
    • Cinemas in Shillong include Bijou Cinema Hall, Gold Cinema and Anjalee Cinema Hall (also called Galleria Anjalee Cinema).
  • Print Media
    • Newspapers are published for Shillong both in Khasi and English. Prominent English dailies published here include Shillong Times, Meghalaya Guardian, Highland Post, Meghalaya Times and The Sentinel. Khasi dailies like U Mawphor, U Nongsaiñ Hima among others are published here. Weekly newspaper are "Salonsar" and "Dongmusa". There are magazines like "Iing Khristan" (100 years of publication), "Pateng Mynta" in Khasi and "Youth Today" and "Eastern Panorama" in English.

Sports

Associations

  • Sports Autharity Of India(SAI)

    • The SAI, Training Centre, Shillong is originally established under SPDA scheme with two sports disciplines namely Athletics & Football. It was started functioning from 5th May’ 1993, in the premises of the Jawaharlal Nehru Sports Complex at Polo Ground, Shillong in assistance with the Govt. of Meghalaya. Since April 2016 STC Shillong Centre has been shifted to SAI SAG Centre, NEHU campus at Shillong

  • Shillong Sports Association

    • The Shillong Sports Association (SSA) was founded in 1916 and was initially known as the Shillong Football Association with Shri Hara Kumar Roy appointed as its first General Secretary . It was later renamed as the Shillong Sports Association in later years. Until the 1950s, the association was mainly involved in organising hockey and football tournaments and competitions but later included other disciplines. The constitution of the association was drafted in 1935 and the Deputy Commissioner of the then United Khasi and Jaintia Hills District was elected as the President of the association. Under the Annual General Meeting of the Shillong Sports Association that was later held in 1958, Shri Jormanik Syiem was elected as the first non-official President.
